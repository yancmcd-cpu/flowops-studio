# FlowOps Studio Brand Style Guide

## 1. Brand Overview

**Brand Purpose**  
FlowOps Studio exists to build automated, high-converting lead capture and follow-up systems for service businesses. We turn operational friction into structured, predictable workflows.

**Brand Tone & Personality**  
*   **Professional & Grounded:** We speak from a place of competence and execution.
*   **Calm & Clear:** Our communication is direct, uncluttered, and easy to understand.
*   **Minimalist & Intentional:** Every visual element and word must serve a specific purpose.
*   **Credible & Authoritative:** We guide clients with quiet confidence, relying on proof over promises.

**What the Brand Avoids**  
*   **Hype & Exaggeration:** No "10x your revenue overnight" claims.
*   **"Startup" Theatrics:** No overly playful, overly casual, or chaotic tech-bro aesthetics.
*   **Trend Chasing:** We do not pivot our visual identity based on the latest web design fads.
*   **Speculative Branding:** We rely on our core, established aesthetic without inventing new, untested directions.
*   **Promotional Fluff:** We avoid dense, salesy paragraphs in favor of outcome-focused clarity.

## 2. Typography

**Primary Font (Headings & Core UI)**  
*   **Inter** (Weights: 600, 700, 800)
*   *Usage:* Used for all major headlines, section titles, and primary calls-to-action to project modern, unwavering stability.

**Secondary Font (Body & Detail)**  
*   **Inter** (Weights: 400, 500)
*   *Usage:* Used for all body copy, descriptive text, labels, and secondary information.

**System Fallbacks**  
*   `system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif`
*   *Usage:* Strictly as a fallback if Inter fails to load, guaranteeing legibility across all operating systems.

---

## 3. Colour Palette

**Primary Colours**  
*   **Background Navy:** `#0B0F14` — Deep, grounding void. Used for all core backgrounds.
*   **Text Primary:** `#E6EDF3` — Soft silver/off-white. Used for high-contrast, readable main text without the harshness of pure white.

**Secondary / Accent Colours**  
*   **Text Secondary:** `#94A3B8` — Slate grey. Used for supporting text, meta-information, and secondary labels.
*   **Accent Cyan:** `#3AAFA9` — Used sparingly for primary actions, active states, and to draw the eye to critical system nodes.
*   **Accent Blue:** `#5B7CFF` — Used for secondary highlights, subtle gradients, and alternating visual structure.

**Background vs Text Usage**  
*   Our aesthetic is strictly **Dark Mode**. Light text (`#E6EDF3`) must always sit on dark backgrounds (`#0B0F14` or dark glass overlays).
*   Borders and structural lines should use subtle white transparency: `rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.08)`.
*   Overlays should use: `rgba(11, 15, 20, 0.75)`.

**Accessibility Guidance**  
*   Ensure text maintains a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 against backgrounds.
*   Do not use `#94A3B8` (Text Secondary) for critical interactive text; reserve it for supporting context only.

## 4. Logo System

**Primary Logo (Stacked / Default)**  
*   The geometric "F" and "O" stylized funnel mark, positioned above or to the left of the "FlowOps Studio" wordmark.

**Horizontal Orientation**  
*   The logo mark aligned horizontally with the wordmark ("FlowOps" in bold, "Studio" in regular/lighter weight). Used for navigation bars and tight vertical constraints.

**Icon-only Mark**  
*   The standalone geometric funnel mark. Used for favicons, profile pictures, and highly constrained spaces.

**Light and Dark Background Variants**  
*   **Dark Background (Primary):** The logo mark retains its `#3AAFA9` and `#5B7CFF` strokes. The wordmark is `#E6EDF3`.
*   **Light Background (Strictly Limited Use):** The logo mark strokes remain intact, but the wordmark shifts to a dark slate (e.g., `#0B0F14`). This is only for external systems that force light mode (e.g., printed documents).

**Clear Space & Minimum Size**  
*   **Clear Space:** Always maintain a clear space around the logo equal to the height of the "F" in the wordmark. No other elements should encroach within this boundary.
*   **Minimum Size:** The wordmark must never be rendered smaller than 16px high; the icon-only mark must never be smaller than 32x32px for legibility of the dual strokes.

## 5. Visual Style

**Whitespace Usage**  
*   High whitespace is mandatory. Elements must breathe.
*   Use significant padding (e.g., `4rem` to `6rem`) between major sections to foster a calm, unstressed reading experience.

**Gradient & Glass Usage**  
*   Maintain the existing subtle dark glassmorphism: translucent panels with a `1px` white border at `8%` opacity (`rgba(255,255,255,0.08)`).
*   Gradients are strictly limited to the neon-like glowing lines connecting system nodes, and faint, deep-background radial spot lights. No harsh, high-contrast linear gradients.

**Do / Don't Guidance**  
*   **DO:** Keep layouts flat, grid-based, and rigidly aligned.
*   **DO:** Rely on typography and spacing, not decorative illustrations, to communicate hierarchy.
*   **DON'T:** Introduce stock photography, cartoons, or complex 3D renders that deviate from our established wireframe/node aesthetic.
*   **DON'T:** Use solid, bright-colored backgrounds for large sections.

---

## 6. Brand Copy Rules

**Headline Style**  
*   Concise, outcome-focused, and title-cased.
*   *Example:* "Turning Interest Into Outcomes" or "Website Lead Capture System".

**Sentence Length Guidance**  
*   Keep sentences under 15 words where possible.
*   Use bullet points for lists; avoid dense, thick paragraphs.

**Words / Phrases to Avoid**  
*   "Synergy", "Hack", "10x", "Crush it", "Revolutionary", "Disruptive".
*   Avoid aggressive sales language ("Buy Now before it's gone"). Use calm instructions ("Start a Conversation", "See The System In Action").
